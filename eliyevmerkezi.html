The Heydar Aliyev Center (Azerbaijani: Heydər Əliyev Mərkəzi) is a landmark cultural complex in Baku, Azerbaijan, renowned for its futuristic architecture and cultural significance. Designed by the late Iraqi-British architect Zaha Hadid, the center exemplifies her signature style of fluid, curvilinear forms that eschew sharp angles.

Architectural Significance

Completed in 2012, the Heydar Aliyev Center spans approximately 57,500 square meters and stands as a symbol of modern Azerbaijan. Its design breaks from the rigid Soviet-era architecture prevalent in Baku, instead embracing a flowing, organic form that reflects the nation's aspirations and cultural identity.
