Where East meets West

Baku is a place where East meets West, tradition meets innovation, and the past speaks through every cobbled street. Wander through the narrow alleyways of Icherisheher, the Old City, and you’ll feel transported back to the days of the Silk Road. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to the Maiden Tower, Shirvanshah’s Palace, and cozy tea houses that invite you to slow down and soak in the atmosphere.

Icherisheher:The Timeless Soul Of Baku

In the heart of modern Baku lies a city within a city—Icherisheher, also known as the Old City. Enclosed by sturdy stone walls and steeped in centuries of history, this ancient quarter is a living museum that captures the soul of Azerbaijan. Walking through the narrow, winding alleys of Icherisheher feels like stepping back in time. Every corner tells a story. The air is filled with the scent of fresh herbs, baked bread, and the soft murmur of stories passed down through generations. At the heart of the Old City stands the legendary Maiden Tower (Qız Qalası), an enigmatic structure shrouded in mystery and folklore. Whether it's a fire-worship temple, a fortress, or a watchtower, no one knows its exact origin—but the view from the top offers a breathtaking panorama of the Caspian Sea and the evolving skyline of Baku. Nearby, the Palace of the Shirvanshahs rises as a symbol of medieval royalty. This majestic complex is 15th-century architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. But Icherisheher is not just about monuments. It's about atmosphere. Local artisans still practice traditional crafts like copper engraving, carpet weaving, and miniature painting. Tiny shops offer handmade souvenirs, and charming cafes invite you to sip Azerbaijani tea.☕︎
